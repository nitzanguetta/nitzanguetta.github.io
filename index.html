Biography

I am a PhD candidate in The Hebrew University of Jeruslaem, Israel, under the supervision of Dr. Roy Shwartz and Dr. Gabriel Stanovsky.

The goal of my research is to improve vision and language generalization. Specifically, I aim to develop models with better compositionality abilities, less biased and better perform on real-world examples. See my publications for more details.

I did my MSc with Prof. Michael Elhadad and Prof. Eitan Bachmat, at the Ben Gurion University.

March 2021 In NAACL 2021
Automatic Generation of Contrast Sets from Scene Graphs

We present a novel method which leverages rich semantic input representation to automatically generate contrast sets for the visual question answering task. Our method computes the answer of perturbed questions, thus vastly reducing annotation cost and enabling thorough evaluation of models' performance on various semantic aspects (e.g., spatial or relational reasoning)

Yonatan Bitton, Raphael Cohen, Tamar Schifter, Eitan Bachmat, Michael Elhadad, Noemie Elhadad
September 2020 In JAMIA 2020
Cross lingual Unified Medical Language System entity linking in online health communities

In Hebrew online health communities, participants commonly write medical terms that appear as transliterated forms of a source term in English. Such transliterations introduce high variability in text and challenge text-analytics methods. To reduce their variability, medical terms must be normalized, such as linking them to Unified Medical Language System (UMLS) concepts. We present a method to identify both transliterated and translated Hebrew medical terms and link them with UMLS entities.
